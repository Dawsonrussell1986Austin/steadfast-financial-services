"How do I get my money back?"

"What happens if a borrower defaults?"

Oak holds the deed of trust. First-lien, senior-secured — our claim sits ahead of every equity holder and every subordinate lender in the stack.

The property is the security.

In 33 full-cycle loans, capital recovery has been 100%. Not because we've been lucky. Because the structure doesn't require luck.

"How is this different from a REIT?"

A REIT makes you the landlord. Oak makes you the bank.

You own the debt, not the building. Your position is senior to every equity holder. If property values fall 30%, equity gets wiped. You don't — because you lent at 60–65% of value.

The cushion is structural, not theoretical.
